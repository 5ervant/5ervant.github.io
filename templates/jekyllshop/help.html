A) Jekyllshop Installation - top

This Jekyll theme is an e-commerce script itself, to generate an awesome static online store with Jekyll. To install it, get the "jekyllshop" folder from the extracted/unzipped "jekyllshop-x.x.x" folder and place it as the theme or as the current directory of your Jekyll site. You can then rename it according to what you want.

The top-level content structure of the unzipped "jekyllshop-x.x.x" folder looks like this:

	.
	├── assets/	<─ Just the assets for the help file.
	├── jekyllshop/	<─ THIS IS THE THEME AND IS WHAT YOU NEED TO GET!
	├── help.html	<─ A help file or this documentation.

Site Settings

And go to the "_config.yml" file of the Jekyllshop theme and set their settings, specially the baseurl and the url settings.

	baseurl: "/yourbaseurl"			# the subpath of your site, e.g. /jekyllshop
	url: "http://www.yourdomain.com"	# the base hostname & protocol for your site

You can find out more info about customizing a Jekyll theme, as well as basic Jekyll usage documentation on their website: jekyllrb.com
If you did not know Jekyll, then you probably need to read their documentation first and learn how they works!

C) Creating Categories - top

You need to create to a category before you can use it on your product. To create a product category, create a Markdown file, for example, such as "sample-category.md" under the "categories/" folder, in this Markdown format:

	---
	layout: category	# Do not change this
	title: Sample Category	# Your awesome category name
	---

	Write an awesome description for your new category here.

That category will now be accessible, for example, at: http://www.yourdomain.com/yourbaseurl/categories/sample-category/

The last directory name of its web address will be based on the category filename which in this example is "sample-category" without the ".md" file extension, and you can create as many categories as you want in the "categories/" folder.

D) Creating Products - top

To create a product item, create a Markdown file with such basename "sample-product.md", inside of the "products/" folder, in these Markdown and YAML formats:

	---
	title:      "Sample Product"
	categories: sample-category				# The previously one that we created
	tags:       sample-tag-1 sample-tag-2
	price:      0.02
	sale:       0.01					# Optional
	stock:      50						# Optional
	date:       2015-12-30					# Optional
	meta:       "Just a sample meta for this product."	# Optional
	author:     YourUsername				# Optional
	---

	Write an awesome Markdown text for your new product here.

That product will now be accessible, e.g. at: http://www.yourdomain.com/yourbaseurl/products/sample-product/
Its web address' last directory name will be based on the product filename "sample-product" without the ".md" extension.

You can use more than one category for your product as long as you created them! Separate them with a space " ", just like tags:

	categories: sample-category another-sample-category
Here are some sample products without images:
  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/products/sample-product-1/
  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/products/sample-product-2/

You do not need to create a tag file. You can use as many tags as you want, separated by a space " ".
A tag name must only have lowercase alphanumeric and a dash, such as "xyz-123".

Here are some sample specified tags' sections:
  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/tags/#tag-1
  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/tags/#nikon

E) Uploading Product Images - top

For example you're going to upload images for your existing "sample-product.md" file, create a folder with a name "sample-product" (the same name as your product filename) under the "products/" folder where your product file is located:

	..
	├── products/sample-product/	<─ The location of your product images.
	├── products/sample-product.md	<─ Your product file.

Inside of the "products/sample-product/" folder, you can upload GIF images and rename them numerically, in the following structure:

	../..
	├── products/sample-product/1.gif
	├── products/sample-product/2.gif
	├── products/sample-product/3.gif
	├──		<─ etc...

Rename them from 1 to the next greater number than it the previous one, increment by increment, and don't miss any next number!
If you will just upload a single GIF image, then just rename it to "1.gif".

  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/products/nikon-d5300/
  • https://5ervant.github.io/templates/jekyllshop/demo/products/sony-nex-6/

F) Specifying Author Details - top

For example, the author variable of your post or product's YAML front matter, is set to "YourUsername", you can then add more details for that specific author inside of the "_data/people.yml" file, in such like this YAML format:

	YourUsername:				# Your author value
	    name: Your Name			# Your real name
	    url: http://www.example.com/	# Your web page address

Those details, your real name with a link to your web page will appear on the meta section of your blog posts and your product items.

For more info, you can visit: jekyllrb.com/docs/datafiles/#example-accessing-a-specific-author

G) Jekyllshop Configuration - top

In the Jekyllshop directory, there you can see a "_config.yml" file. In that file you can configure your Jekyllshop, just visit that configuration file and set the right settings and payment providers for your online shop.

For Examples

  • You need to disable the BACS a.k.a. direct bank transfer method, then change the bacs_enabled setting to 0: 
    	bacs_enabled:        0
  • To change your currency, e.g. to Philippine peso, then change the currency_code and the currency_symbol like these: 
    	currency_code:   PHP
	currency_symbol: ₱
  • To configure your PayPal email, set the paypal_email setting to your PayPal email address: 
    	paypal_email:       your-paypal-email@example.com
  • To disable an "Add to Cart" button or the shopping cart system, change the no_cart_button setting to 1: 
    	no_cart_button:  1

And there's a lot more that you can do, just visit the "_config.yml" file and change the settings according to what you want, they are well-commented and easy to understand.

You can either leave an optional setting blank (without anything even quotes) if you want to exclude it..
"How would I know if it's an optional setting or not?" Simply, if you set it to blank and the site couldn't build, means, that setting is required.

To learn how to set the core configuration of a Jekyll site, please visit: jekyllrb.com/docs/configuration/#default-configuration

I) Creating Pages - top

To create a page, simply create a Markdown file, e.g. "sample-page.md", under the Jekyllshop theme's directory, in this format:

	---
	layout: page			# Do not change this
	title: Sample Page
	permalink: /sample-page/	# Will be its URL's last directory name
	glyphicon: glyphicon-asterisk	# Will be its page link's icon
	---
	
	Write an awesome Markdown text for your new page here.

Your page will now be accessible, e.g. at http://www.yourdomain.com/yourbaseurl/sample-page/ with its web address' last directory name as the assigned value "/sample-page/" to its YAML front matter's permalink variable.

Page Link's Glyphicon

To find a right Glyphicon for your page, simply go to getbootstrap.com/components/#glyphicons-glyphs and choose the glyph that you want.

For example, you chose the "glyphicon glyphicon-plus" icon, copy its succeeded text "glyphicon-plus" and put it as the value of the glyphicon variable of your page's YAML front matter, such like this:

	glyphicon: glyphicon-plus

After doing that, your page link that's listed on your site navigation will now have a "glyphicon-plus" icon.

Unlist and Nofollow a Page

If you don't want to list your page on your site navigation, just put the line "robots: nofollow" on its YAML front matter.

	---
	layout: page
	title: Sample Page
	permalink: /sample-page/
	robots: nofollow
	---
	
	Write an awesome Markdown text for your new page here.

Your page link will now be unlisted on your site navigation. And also, search engines will not list that page in their search results.

J) Uploading Logo, Header and Favicon - top

To upload a logo for your site, simply just upload it to the root directory of your Jekyllshop site and rename it as exactly as "logo.gif".

To upload a "favicon.ico", just also upload it to the root directory. It needs a basename as exactly as "favicon.ico".

You can also delete the "logo.gif" and the "favicon.ico" if you want. If the "logo.gif" is missing, your site's title will be shown in the logo area.

To upload a header image, upload a "header"-named GIF image into your root directory. It needs a basename as exactly as "header.gif".

L) Widgets and Plugins - top

You do not need to worry about some other Jekyllshop's predefined widgets and plugins because:

  • Once you've created some products, they will be automatically listed in the Products Vertical and Products Horizontal widgets that are pre-installed on your Jekyllshop.
  • And once your products have their own images, their images will be shown in the Intro-Carousel plugin, the Products Vertical widget and in the Products Horizontal widget.
  • The Search (Engine) widget can start to find a result with or without an image, once you've a created product. And a lot more!

N) For Child Theme Developers - top

For those who are willing to develop a child theme for making the design of a Jekyllshop more attractive, either for free or for commercial, the most allowable contents to redistribute to install into a Jekyllshop theme if you have the license are these folders:

	├── _data/
	├── _includes/
	├── _layouts/
	├── _sass/
	├── assets/images/
	├── css/

An instant and standalone child theme that already has the working Jekyllshop system in itself, is prohibited and not permitted!
